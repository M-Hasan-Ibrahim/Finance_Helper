Chapter 7

Eco-design Standards and Web Measurement

Combining legal standards, technical measurement, and simple design choices to build useful digital services with fewer resources.

1. Legal Framework

2020AGEC Law limits software obsolescence by separating security updates from optional updates.
2021REEN Law promotes education, reuse, refurbishment, and monitoring of digital impacts.
2024RGESN was integrated into the REEN Law.
InternationalW3C Web Sustainability Guidelines provide recommendations for sustainable websites.

Other important guides include AFNOR SPEC, GR491, GreenIT's 115 practices, Opquast, and the Responsible Digital Marketing Framework.

2. The RGESN Framework

RGESN means General Framework for the Eco-design of Digital Services. It complements other major French digital standards:

RGAAAccessibility
RGPDPersonal-data protection
RGSSecurity
RGIInteroperability
RGESNDigital eco-design

4. Measuring Environmental Performance

Eco-design follows a continuous improvement process:

Measure→ Eco-design→ Implement→ Measure improvements→ Operate

Measurement establishes the initial state, identifies the most harmful elements, and verifies whether improvements are effective.

EcoIndexGreenIT AnalysisCarbonalyserKastorWebsite CarbonEcoGrader

Different tools may assess the frontend, network, backend, cloud, or servers. Their scope must therefore be understood before comparing results.

5. Essential Web Metrics

Network requestsExchanges required to load HTML, scripts, images, fonts, and other resources.
Decoded dataThe complete resource size after decompression.
Transferred dataThe amount sent through the network, including HTTP headers.

Network compression

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, JSON, and SVG can usually be compressed during transfer.

Direct optimization

Images, audio, video, fonts, PDFs, and ZIP files generally need the files themselves to be optimized.

6. Designing a Sober Website

1

Identify resource-intensive features.

2

Remove unnecessary content and functions.

3

Reduce requests and transferred data.

4

Improve usability and accessibility.

5

Simplify navigation and user journeys.

6

Measure the results after implementation.

Proportionate technology

A complex CMS, artificial intelligence, or a heavy framework is not always necessary. Technology should remain proportional to the actual need.

7. Focusing on the Essential

Every digital service should define its functional unit: the actual service delivered, such as buying a ticket, reading an article, or contacting an organization.

Display the right function at the right moment
Avoid presenting every possible feature
Reduce the steps needed to complete a task
Follow a mobile-first approach
Provide direct and relevant content
Help users achieve their goal and leave quickly

Main Idea

Digital eco-design combines legal standards, technical measurement, and simple design decisions. A sustainable website uses proportionate technology, provides only useful functions, and helps users achieve their objectives with minimal resources.
